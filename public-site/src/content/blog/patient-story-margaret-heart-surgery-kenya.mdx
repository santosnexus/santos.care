"I remember the exact moment everything changed. I was climbing the stairs to my daughter's apartment in Nairobi — just one flight — and I had to stop twice to catch my breath. My chest was tight, my left arm ached, and I knew something was very wrong."

Margaret is 67, a retired nurse from Kenya. She spent 35 years working in hospitals across Nairobi, caring for others. She never expected to become the patient.

---

## The Diagnosis

After an ECG and echocardiogram at a Nairobi hospital, the cardiologist didn't mince words: "You have triple vessel disease. You need a coronary artery bypass graft — and you need it soon."

The quote for surgery at a private hospital in Nairobi: KES 2.5 million ($19,500).

"I sat in my car in the hospital parking lot and cried," Margaret remembers. "Not because I was scared of the surgery — I'd seen hundreds of patients go through it. Because I didn't have that kind of money, and I didn't know where to turn."

The public hospital waiting list was 8–11 months. Her cardiologist made it clear she didn't have that long.

---

## Discovering Another Path

A friend from her church had travelled to India for knee replacement surgery the previous year. She told Margaret about Heal India.

"I was sceptical at first. I'd spent my career in Kenyan hospitals. I believed in our system. But my friend showed me photos of the hospital in Kochi — and the price she paid. I decided to at least make the call."

Within 24 hours of sending her medical reports via WhatsApp, Margaret received a detailed treatment plan from Aster Medcity in Kochi — one of India's leading cardiac centres. The quote: $8,500 for full CABG surgery, including a 6-day hospital stay, ICU, surgeon fees, and all medications.

*Even after adding flights, visa, and 3 weeks of accommodation for her daughter who accompanied her, the total was under $11,000 — less than half of the Nairobi quote.*

---

## The Journey

**Day 1 — Arrival in Kochi**

Margaret and her daughter flew Nairobi–Addis Ababa–Kochi with Ethiopian Airlines. Total travel time: 9 hours. A Heal India coordinator met them at arrivals with a signboard and drove them directly to the hospital.

"The coordinator spoke perfect English. She had our paperwork ready, our room prepared, and a schedule for the next day's tests. I was exhausted from the flight, but I felt — for the first time since my diagnosis — that everything was going to be okay."

**Day 2–3 — Pre-Operative Workup**

Full blood work, echocardiogram, coronary angiogram, anaesthetist evaluation. The cardiologist and cardiac surgeon reviewed her case together.

"The surgeon spent 45 minutes with us, explaining exactly what he would do, what the risks were, and what my recovery would look like. He'd trained at a hospital in London and performed over 500 bypass surgeries. I trusted him completely."

**Day 4 — Surgery Day**

Margaret underwent a 4-hour coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) — three grafts. She was in the cardiac ICU for 48 hours.

"I woke up in the ICU with my daughter holding my hand. The nurses checked on me every 15 minutes. The ICU had one nurse for every two patients — better ratios than any hospital I'd worked in."

**Day 5–9 — Recovery in Hospital**

Moved to a private room. Daily physiotherapy, breathing exercises, wound care.

"On day 6, the physiotherapist had me walking to the window. On day 8, I walked the length of the corridor. The progress surprised even me."

**Day 10–21 — Ayurvedic Recovery**

Margaret transferred to Ayush Prana, the Ayurvedic centre on the banks of the Periyar River, for 12 days of post-surgery rehabilitation.

"I'd never experienced Ayurveda before. Gentle medicated oil massages, herbal teas, yoga tailored for post-cardiac patients. The inflammation in my chest reduced noticeably. My energy came back faster than I expected."

**Day 22 — Return to Nairobi**

Discharge summary, medication plan for 3 months, and a scheduled video follow-up with her surgeon in 4 weeks. The coordinator accompanied them to the airport.
