Living with Multiple Sclerosis means facing not just the physical and emotional challenges of the condition, but also the financial reality of lifelong treatment. Disease-modifying therapies (DMTs) in the US can cost $60,000–$100,000 per year. In the UK, while NHS covers DMTs, access to advanced treatments like HSCT (hematopoietic stem cell transplant) or intensive rehabilitation is limited.

India offers a compelling alternative — not as a compromise, but as a pathway to affordable, world-class MS care that combines cutting-edge medical treatment with holistic Ayurvedic rehabilitation in Kerala.

## Why India Is Emerging as a Global MS Treatment Destination

### 1. Stem Cell Therapy at World-Class Standards

India's top hematology and neurology centers perform HSCT for MS using the same protocols as leading US and European hospitals. The procedure involves harvesting the patient's own stem cells, administering high-dose chemotherapy to reset the immune system, and reinfusing the stem cells to rebuild immunity.

Hospitals like Aster Medcity (Kochi), Apollo Hospitals (Chennai), and Max Healthcare (Delhi) have performed hundreds of HSCT procedures for MS patients from over 30 countries. Success rates — defined as no disease activity for 3+ years — range from 65–80% for relapsing-remitting MS, consistent with international benchmarks.

### 2. Ayurvedic MS Care — A Unique Differentiator

Kerala is home to **Ayush Prana**, the world's only Ayurvedic Multiple Sclerosis specialty hospital. Located on the banks of the Periyar River, 25 km from Kochi International Airport, Ayush Prana has treated MS patients exclusively since 2001.

Their approach combines:
- Custom herbal formulations prepared in an in-house GMP-certified pharmacy
- Personalized detoxification (Panchakarma) therapies
- Dietary and lifestyle modifications
- Yoga and meditation for neurological support
- Remote follow-up care after patients return home

Many patients combine conventional DMT or HSCT treatment with a 3–4 week Ayurvedic recovery program at Ayush Prana — a treatment model unique to Kerala.

### 3. Cost-Effective Long-Term Care

For relapsing-remitting MS patients who need ongoing DMTs, the cost savings in India are transformative. A year of Ocrevus in India costs approximately $15,000 compared to $65,000 in the US. Even after adding travel and accommodation, the total is typically 60–75% less.
