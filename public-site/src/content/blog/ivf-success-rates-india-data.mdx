## Factors That Influence IVF Success in India

### 1. Embryo Testing (PGT-A/PGT-M)

Many Indian fertility clinics offer pre-implantation genetic testing (PGT) — screening embryos for chromosomal abnormalities before transfer. This increases the chance of a successful pregnancy, particularly for women over 38.

### 2. Time-Lapse Embryo Monitoring

EmbryoScope and similar time-lapse systems allow embryologists to select the most viable embryos without disturbing the culture environment — leading to higher implantation rates.

### 3. Vitrification (Flash-Freezing)

Indian clinics use vitrification for embryo freezing — a rapid freezing technique that achieves over 95% thaw survival rates, comparable to the best global standards.

### 4. Cumulative Cycle Affordability

This is India's unique advantage. Three IVF cycles in India ($7,500–$15,000) cost less than one cycle in most Western countries. The ability to attempt multiple cycles dramatically improves cumulative success rates.

## How to Compare IVF Success Rates

When evaluating fertility clinics, ask for:

| Data Point | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Age-stratified success rates | A clinic's overall rate is meaningless if it only treats young patients |
| Fresh vs frozen embryo transfer rates | Some clinics achieve better results with one approach |
| Single embryo transfer (SET) rate | High SET rates indicate good lab quality (selecting the best embryo) |
| Multiple pregnancy rate | Lower is better — multiple pregnancies carry higher risks |
| Cancellation rate | High cancellation rates may indicate aggressive stimulation protocols |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do Indian clinics report success rates honestly?**
A: JCI and NABH-accredited clinics are required to track and report outcomes. We recommend asking for age-stratified data from your shortlisted clinic.

**Q: Is the success rate for frozen embryo transfers as good as fresh?**
A: Yes. With vitrification technology, frozen embryo transfer success rates are comparable to — and sometimes better than — fresh transfers.

**Q: How many cycles should I plan for?**
A: Most patients plan for 2–3 cycles. The cumulative success rate after 3 cycles is significantly higher than a single cycle.

**Q: Does PGT-A testing improve success rates?**
A: For women over 38, yes. PGT-A screens for chromosomal abnormalities, reducing the risk of failed implantation and miscarriage.
