I'm a 62-year-old retired teacher from Florida. For three years, I lived with hip pain that got worse every month. Walking the dog became a challenge. Getting out of the car required a strategy. Sleep was a distant memory.

My orthopaedic surgeon in the US was excellent. His diagnosis was clear: advanced osteoarthritis of the right hip. His recommendation: total hip replacement. And his quote: **$52,000** — my out-of-pocket cost after insurance.

I sat in my car and cried. Not because I was scared of surgery, but because I couldn't afford it.

---

## The Discovery

A friend at church mentioned that her cousin had gone to India for knee replacement. "She paid $6,000," my friend said. "Including the hospital stay."

I was sceptical. India? For surgery? I'd never even been outside the United States.

But I was also desperate. So I went home, searched online, and found Heal India Medi Tourism. I sent them a WhatsApp message at 9 PM. By 10 AM the next morning, I had a treatment plan from a hospital in Kochi, India — with a quote of **$5,800 for the same hip replacement**.

The same Zimmer Biomet implant I'd been quoted in Florida. A JCI-accredited hospital. A surgeon who'd done 400+ hip replacements per year. Total cost: $5,800.

---

## The Decision

I spent two weeks researching, reading reviews, and talking to the hospital's international patient coordinator. I verified their JCI accreditation on the official website. I even had a video call with the surgeon.

Every question I asked was answered clearly and honestly. No pressure. No rush. Just facts.

I booked my flights.

## The Journey

### Arrival

The 22-hour journey from Florida to Kochi involved one stop in Dubai. A coordinator met me at the airport with a sign — just like they'd promised. We drove 30 minutes to the hospital, and I was checked into my private room within an hour of landing.

### Pre-Op

The next two days were pre-operative tests: blood work, ECG, X-rays, and a final consultation with the surgeon. He spent 45 minutes explaining the procedure, the implant, and the recovery plan. In Florida, I'd gotten 12 minutes with my surgeon.

### Surgery

Total hip replacement. Three hours in the operating room. Robotic-assisted for precision implant placement. I was walking (with a walker) the same evening.

### Hospital Stay

Five days in a private room with a nurse available at the touch of a button. Daily physiotherapy. Good food. My coordinator checked in twice a day.

### Ayurvedic Recovery

This part I hadn't planned for — but it turned out to be the highlight of the trip. I transferred to Ayush Prana, an Ayurvedic centre on a riverbank near Kochi, for 10 days of post-surgery rehabilitation.

Gentle oil massages, herbal treatments, yoga adapted for my recovery, and fresh Kerala cuisine. The inflammation in my hip reduced noticeably. My energy returned. I walked out of Ayush Prana using just a cane — two weeks ahead of the typical recovery timeline for my age.
