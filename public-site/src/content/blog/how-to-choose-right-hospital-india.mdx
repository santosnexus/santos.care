India has over 50,000 registered hospitals. Fewer than 40 hold JCI accreditation — the global gold standard. Hundreds more hold NABH accreditation, India's national quality benchmark. And thousands have neither.

For an international patient flying thousands of kilometres for treatment, choosing the right hospital is the single most important decision you'll make. This guide walks you through exactly what to look for, what questions to ask, and how to verify everything independently.

---

## The 10-Step Hospital Selection Checklist

### Step 1: Check Accreditation — JCI or NABH

This is non-negotiable. Accreditation means an independent body has verified the hospital meets defined safety and quality standards.

| Accreditation | What It Means | How to Verify |
|---|---|---|
| **JCI** (Joint Commission International) | Global gold standard. Same standards as top US hospitals. | Visit jointcommissioninternational.org → Find Accredited Organizations |
| **NABH Full** (National Accreditation Board for Hospitals) | India's national quality benchmark. Strong credential. | Visit nabh.co → Accredited Organizations |
| **NABH Entry Level** | Basic certification. Acceptable but less rigorous. | Same NABH website |

**Red flag:** "In the process of applying for accreditation" or "JCI-equivalent" are not the same as being accredited.

### Step 2: Verify Surgeon Credentials

Your surgeon matters more than the hospital name. Ask for:

- Full name and medical council registration number
- Sub-specialty fellowship training — where did they train? (Mayo Clinic, Johns Hopkins, King's College London?)
- Annual surgical volume — how many of *your specific procedure* do they perform per year?
- Does the surgeon operate personally, or will a resident/fellow perform parts of the surgery?

A good surgeon will share their CV and credentials openly. A vague response is a red flag.

### Step 3: Ask for Outcome Data

Reputable hospitals track and publish their outcomes. Ask:

- What is your complication rate for this specific procedure?
- What is your infection rate (surgical site infection)?
- What is the success rate for my type of surgery?

**Acceptable answer:** "Our CABG mortality rate is under 1.5%, consistent with international benchmarks."
**Vague answer:** "We have excellent outcomes" (without data to support it).

### Step 4: Get an Itemised, Written Quote

Package pricing varies significantly between hospitals. A $10,000 knee replacement at one hospital may include the implant; at another, the implant may be extra.

Ask for a line-by-line breakdown:

| Component | Should Be Included? |
|---|---|
| Pre-operative tests | ✅ |
| Surgeon fee | ✅ |
| Anaesthetist fee | ✅ |
| Implant/device cost | ✅ |
| Hospital stay (specify days) | ✅ |
| ICU stay (if needed) | ✅ |
| Physiotherapy | ✅ |
| Medications during stay | ✅ |
| Follow-up consultations | ✅ |
| Airport transfer | Usually included |
| Coordinator services | Usually included |

### Step 5: Evaluate the International Patient Department

A hospital experienced with international patients will have:

- Dedicated international patient coordinator (24/7 availability)
- Multi-lingual staff (English is standard; Arabic, Swahili, French available at top hospitals)
- Assistance with visa invitation letters
- Airport pickup and drop-off
- Currency exchange or international payment support

### Step 6: Understand the Post-Discharge Follow-Up Plan

What happens after you leave the hospital?

| Component | Standard | Good Practice |
|---|---|---|
| In-person follow-up | 1 visit before discharge | 2–3 scheduled appointments |
| Physiotherapy plan | Verbal instructions | Written plan with milestones |
| Telemedicine follow-up | Not offered | 3–6 months free follow-up |
| 24/7 WhatsApp access | Limited | Direct line to your coordinator |

### Step 7: Check Infection Control Protocols

Post-operative infection is a serious risk. Accredited hospitals maintain documented infection control programmes. Ask:

- What is your surgical site infection rate?
- How do you monitor hand hygiene compliance?
- What sterilisation protocols do you use?

JCI and NABH hospitals are required to track and publish this data. If the hospital can't or won't share it, that's a concern.

### Step 8: Ask for Patient References

A hospital confident in its care will connect you with past international patients who had similar procedures. While privacy laws limit what they can share, most will arrange a phone or WhatsApp conversation with a willing former patient.

### Step 9: Compare 2–3 Hospitals

Don't commit to the first hospital you research. Get quotes and credentials from at least 2–3 hospitals. Use a comparison table:

| Criteria | Hospital A | Hospital B | Hospital C |
|---|---|---|---|
| Accreditation | JCI (2025) | NABH (2024) | NABH only |
| Surgeon volume | 420/year | 280/year | 90/year |
| Complication rate | 1.3% | 1.8% | Not shared |
| Package breakdown | Written, detailed | Verbal only | Verbal, vague |
| Telemedicine follow-up | 6 months free | Paid | Not offered |

### Step 10: Trust Your Instincts

If a hospital is pushy, evasive about pricing, or unwilling to share credentials, move on. The best hospitals for international patients are transparent, patient, and communicative.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is NABH accreditation enough, or should I insist on JCI?**
A: Both are excellent. JCI is the global gold standard, but NABH Full Accreditation is a rigorous, well-respected benchmark. For most procedures, either is sufficient.

**Q: Should I choose the cheapest hospital?**
A: No. The cheapest option often excludes implants, physiotherapy, or follow-up care. Compare like-for-like packages, not just headline prices.

**Q: How many hospitals should I compare?**
A: 2–3 is sufficient. More than that becomes overwhelming without additional benefit.

**Q: Can I visit the hospital before deciding?**
A: If you're already in India, yes. If not, we arrange a virtual tour via video call.
