This guide compares the three most common orthopedic procedures international patients seek in India: hip replacement, knee replacement, and spine surgery.

## Hip Replacement

### Best Candidates
Patients with advanced hip osteoarthritis, avascular necrosis, or hip fractures that haven't healed properly. Typical age range: 50–80 years.

### Surgical Approaches Available in India

| Approach | Incision Size | Hospital Stay | Recovery |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traditional posterior | 6–8 inches | 4–6 days | 6–12 weeks |
| Anterior approach (muscle-sparing) | 3–4 inches | 2–4 days | 4–8 weeks |
| Robotic-assisted | 3–4 inches | 2–4 days | 4–8 weeks |

### Implant Options

Same global brands as US and European hospitals:
- **Zimmer Biomet** (most common)
- **Stryker** (including Mako robotic-arm assisted)
- **DePuy Synthes** (Johnson & Johnson)
- **Smith & Nephew**

### Recovery Milestones

| Timeframe | Milestone |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Stand with support |
| Week 1 | Walk with walker |
| Week 2–3 | Walk with cane |
| Week 6 | Drive (right hip) |
| Month 3 | Full daily activities |

## Knee Replacement

### Best Candidates
Patients with advanced knee osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, or post-traumatic arthritis where conservative treatments have failed.

### Types of Knee Replacement

| Type | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Total Knee Replacement (TKR) | All three compartments replaced | Advanced arthritis in multiple compartments |
| Partial Knee Replacement | Single compartment replaced | Arthritis limited to one area |
| Robotic-Assisted TKR | Computer-guided precision alignment | All patients (better long-term alignment) |
| Bilateral TKR | Both knees in one surgery | Patients with both knees affected |

### Robotic-Assisted Advantage

Hospitals in Kochi, Delhi, and Chennai offer robotic-assisted knee replacement using systems like Stryker Mako and Zimmer ROSA. Benefits:

- Sub-millimetre implant positioning accuracy
- More natural knee movement post-surgery
- Longer implant longevity (better alignment = less wear)
- Faster recovery (average hospital stay: 3–4 days vs 5–7 days for traditional)

## Spine Surgery

### Best Candidates
Patients with herniated discs, spinal stenosis, spondylolisthesis, scoliosis, or degenerative disc disease who haven't improved with conservative treatment.

### Common Procedures

| Procedure | What It Treats | Hospital Stay |
|---|---|---|
| Microdiscectomy | Herniated disc | 1–2 days |
| Laminectomy | Spinal stenosis | 2–4 days |
| Spinal Fusion (TLIF/PLIF) | Instability, spondylolisthesis | 3–5 days |
| Cervical Fusion | Neck disc herniation | 2–4 days |
| Scoliosis Correction | Curvature deformity | 5–7 days |

### Minimally Invasive Options

Most spine surgeries in India are now performed using minimally invasive techniques — smaller incisions, less muscle damage, and faster recovery than traditional open surgery.
