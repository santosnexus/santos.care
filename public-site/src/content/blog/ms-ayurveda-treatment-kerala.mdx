## The Ayurvedic Approach to MS

In Ayurveda, MS is understood primarily as a **Vata disorder** — a condition where the nervous system's regulatory functions are disrupted, leading to degeneration of myelin and impaired nerve signal transmission.

| Ayurvedic Principle | How It Applies to MS |
|---|---|
| **Vata pacification** | Therapies to calm the nervous system and reduce symptoms |
| **Rasayana (rejuvenation)** | Herbal formulations to support nerve regeneration and myelin repair |
| **Panchakarma (detoxification)** | Gentle elimination of metabolic toxins that may trigger inflammation |
| **Agni (digestive fire)** | Dietary protocols to reduce systemic inflammation |
| **Sattvic lifestyle** | Mental and emotional balance through meditation and yoga |

## How Ayurveda Complements Conventional MS Treatment

The most effective MS care often combines the best of both worlds:

| Treatment | What It Does Best | Limitations |
|---|---|---|
| DMTs (Ocrevus, Tysabri, etc.) | Reduce relapse rate, slow disability progression | Costly, side effects, don't address all symptoms |
| Symptom management (medications) | Target specific symptoms (spasticity, pain, bladder issues) | Often partial relief |
| Physiotherapy & rehabilitation | Maintain mobility and function | Requires ongoing commitment |
| **Ayurveda (Ayush Prana)** | Reduces fatigue, improves energy, addresses inflammation, provides stress management | Complementary — not a replacement for DMTs |
