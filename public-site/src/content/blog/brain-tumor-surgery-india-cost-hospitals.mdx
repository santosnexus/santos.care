## Advanced Neurosurgical Techniques Available in India

### 1. Intraoperative MRI (iMRI)

Hospitals like Aster Medcity (Kochi) and Max Healthcare (Delhi) use intraoperative MRI during tumour resection surgeries. The surgeon scans the brain during the procedure to confirm complete tumour removal — reducing the need for repeat surgeries.

### 2. Gamma Knife / CyberKnife Radiosurgery

For small to medium tumours in difficult-to-reach locations, non-invasive radiosurgery delivers targeted radiation with sub-millimetre precision. No incision, no hospital stay — often completed as a day procedure.

### 3. Neuro-Navigation & Awake Craniotomy

For tumours near critical brain regions (speech, motor function), surgeons use neuro-navigation systems and awake craniotomy techniques — the patient is awake during part of the surgery so the surgeon can map and preserve vital functions.

### 4. Endoscopic Endonasal Surgery

For pituitary tumours and skull base tumours, surgeons access the tumour through the nasal passages — no visible scar, faster recovery, shorter hospital stay.

## Navigating the Pathology Report

After tumour resection, the biopsy is sent for histopathological analysis. This determines:

- **Tumour type** (meningioma, glioma, schwannoma, metastatic, etc.)
- **Grade** (WHO Grade I–IV)
- **Molecular markers** (IDH mutation, MGMT methylation, 1p/19q codeletion)

These results guide the next steps — whether additional treatment (radiation, chemotherapy) is needed or surveillance is sufficient. At top Indian hospitals, the pathology turnaround is 7–10 days.
