## 1. Why India? The Case for Medical Tourism

### 1.1 The Global Medical Tourism Imperative

Medical tourism has evolved from a niche market to a significant global industry driven by four fundamental forces: **cost arbitrage**, **quality accessibility**, **technology adoption**, and **regulatory enablement**. India stands at the convergence of all four forces, making it one of the most attractive destinations for international patients.

### 1.3 Government Policy Support

The Indian government has created a robust policy framework to support medical tourism:

**Heal in India Initiative (2022-present)**
- Streamlined e-Medical Visa for 170+ countries
- 48-72 hour approval cycle
- Valid for 6 months with multiple entries
- Companion e-Medical Attendant Visa available

**Ayush Visa (August 2023)**
- New visa category for traditional medicine seekers
- Targets wellness and Ayurveda tourism
- Complements clinical medical tourism

**National Medical & Wellness Tourism Board**
- Coordinates public-private partnerships
- Quality assurance and accreditation standards
- International marketing and promotion

## 9. Risk Assessment & Mitigation

**Table 9.1: Risk Matrix**

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| **Geopolitical Instability** (source markets) | Medium | High | Diversify across 5+ source countries |
| **Margin Compression** (AI-driven competitors) | High | Medium | Deepen concierge services (hard to replicate) |
| **Reputational Risk** (one bad outcome) | Low | Very High | Rigorous hospital vetting, malpractice insurance |
| **Regulatory Changes** (visa, medical) | Medium | Medium | Stay updated, government relations |
| **Ayurveda Partner Failure** | Medium (if single partner) | High | Maintain 3-4 accredited partner options |
| **Currency Fluctuation** | Medium | Low | Price in USD/EUR for international patients |
| **Competitor Entry** (local Kerala) | Medium | Medium | Build brand, patient testimonials, partnerships |
| **Hospital Commission Reduction** | Medium | Medium | Diversify revenue (travel, wellness, insurance) |
| **Technology Failure** (platform, CRM) | Low | High | Redundant systems, backups, manual fallback |
| **Patient Safety Incident** | Low | Very High | Insurance, indemnity, strict SOPs |
